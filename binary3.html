How do computers know anything?

So you have learned about circuits! Now, I told you
that when a circuit is on, the computer sees that the
number 1, and a 0 when it is off. Now I want you to
think a little about human numbers, also know as the
decimal system.

I know, I know. That name sounds a little fancy, but all
it really means is that all our numbers are made by combining
the same 10 numbers in different ways. Think how 2, 6 and 3 go
together to form the number 263!
Now think about it. In that number, 2 really represents 200, right?
And 6 representes 60, and 3... Well 3 just represents 3.
I know, terribly boring ending, but wait! There's more!
So, 263 can be thought of as, 200 + 60 + 3, which is the same
as 2 * 100 + 6 * 10 + 3 * 1! Notice something? As we're going
to the right, the number we're multiplying by gets divided by
10! It goes 1, 10, 100, 1000, 10000 all the way to infinity!
This is because we are using a 10 number system, so all we have
to do in a 2 number system is change that number to 2! Let me
show you what happens!
