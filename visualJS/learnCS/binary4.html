How do computers know anything?

So with the decimal system(that's us) we can write 55 as 5 * 10 + 5 * 1. Now, what if I told you that the 2 number system version of that number is 110111? Wait. That looks like a decimal number! But it's not! Remember, the computer can only use 1 and 0 for numbers, so we've had to get a little bit creative to get our numbers into them. So in the two number system, often called binary, we can rewrite numbers like we do in decimal, but we use 2 instead! So let's see... Starting from the right. The 1 is still just 1, = 1 The next 1 is 1 * 2 = 2 The next 1 is 1 * 2 * 2 = 4 The next 0 is 0 * 2 * 2 * 2 = 0 The next 1 is 1 * 2 * 2 * 2 * 2 = 16 The next 1 is 1 * 2 * 2 * 2 * 2 * 2 = 32 Which totals to = 55 How about that? So just by counting circuits that are on and off, we can now represents real numbers in the computer!
